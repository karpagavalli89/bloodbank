What to Expect

Donating blood is a safe, simple, and incredibly rewarding process. From the moment you walk through our doors to the time you leave, the entire process takes about 45 minutes to an hour, but the actual donation only takes 8-10 minutes.

See the Steps ↓

The 4-Step Process

We ensure every step is comfortable and meets the highest medical safety standards.

1

Registration

Our staff will welcome you. You will sign in, show a valid ID, and read some important information about donating blood. We will also ask you to complete a private medical history questionnaire.

2

Mini-Physical

A trained professional will check your temperature, blood pressure, pulse, and hemoglobin levels in a private setting to ensure it is completely safe for you to donate today.

3

The Donation

You will be seated comfortably. We will cleanse your arm and use a brand-new, sterile needle. The actual drawing of blood takes only 8 to 10 minutes. You can relax, listen to music, or talk to our staff.

4

Refreshments

After donating, you will rest in our observation area for 10-15 minutes. We will provide you with water, juice, and snacks to help your body begin replenishing fluids immediately.

Post-Donation Care

Taking care of yourself after donating is just as important as the donation itself. Follow these guidelines for the rest of the day.

  • 💧 Hydrate: Drink an extra 4 glasses (32 oz) of non-alcoholic liquids.
  • 🩹 Keep the Bandage On: Leave the bandage on for the next several hours to prevent bruising.
  • 🏋️ Avoid Heavy Lifting: Do not do any heavy lifting or vigorous exercise for the rest of the day.
  • 🧘 Rest if Dizzy: If you feel lightheaded, lie down with your feet elevated until the feeling passes.

Common Questions

Does donating blood hurt?

You will feel a brief pinch when the needle goes in, but there should be no pain during the donation itself.

How much blood is taken?

We collect approximately one pint (about 470 ml) of blood, which is roughly 8-10% of the average adult's blood volume.

How long does it take my body to replace it?

Your body replaces the lost plasma within 24 hours. Red blood cells take about 4 to 6 weeks to be fully replenished.

Is the equipment safe?

Absolutely. All needles and equipment used are pre-packaged, sterile, and used only once before being safely discarded.
