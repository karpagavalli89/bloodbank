Can I Donate Blood?

Giving blood is a uniquely powerful act, but the safety of both the donor and the recipient is our top priority. Most healthy adults can donate, but there are some specific guidelines you must meet.

View Requirements ↓

The Basics

To ensure a safe donation experience, all donors must meet these foundational health criteria before sitting in the donor chair.

🎂

Age

You must be between 18 and 65 years old. (16 or 17-year-olds may donate with signed parental consent in some regions).

⚖️

Weight

You must weigh at least 50 kg (110 lbs). This ensures your blood volume is sufficient to safely handle a one-pint donation.

❤️

General Health

You must be feeling well and healthy on the day of donation. No colds, flu, fevers, or active infections.

⏱️

Frequency

You must wait at least 56 days (8 weeks) between whole blood donations to allow your body to replenish its supply.

Common Deferrals

Sometimes you might be temporarily or permanently restricted from donating to protect your health or the blood supply.

⏳ Temporary Deferrals

You will need to wait a specific amount of time before donating if you have:

  • Gotten a new tattoo or piercing (wait 3-6 months depending on state laws).
  • Traveled to malaria-risk countries (wait 3 months).
  • Had a minor dental procedure (wait 24-72 hours).
  • Taken certain antibiotics or medications (wait until finished).
  • Been pregnant (wait 6 weeks post-pregnancy).

🚫 Permanent Deferrals

You may not be eligible to donate blood at any time if you have:

  • Ever tested positive for HIV/AIDS.
  • A history of Hepatitis B or Hepatitis C after age 11.
  • Certain types of blood cancers (leukemia, lymphoma).
  • Taken certain medications (like Tegison for psoriasis).

Day-of-Donation Checklist

If you meet all the criteria above, make sure you are prepared for your appointment today!

  • ✅ Eat a healthy meal: Avoid fatty foods like hamburgers or ice cream before donating.
  • ✅ Hydrate well: Drink an extra 16 oz of water before your appointment.
  • ✅ Bring your ID: A driver's license, passport, or two other forms of identification.
  • ✅ Wear comfortable clothing: Wear a shirt with sleeves that can easily be rolled up above the elbow.
  • ✅ Bring a list of medications: Know the names of any prescriptions you are currently taking.
